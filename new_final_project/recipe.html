Almond pavlova

This naturally gluten free treat has a few new twists.

words and photos EMMI-LIIA SJÖHOLM
original recipe PIA ANDERSSON
the winter edition JANUARY 2016

FOR THE MERINGUE
4 (organic) egg whites
250 g cane sugar
2 tsp corn flour
1 tsp white wine vinegar
1 tbsp sesame oil
50 g almond crumbs

FOR THE TOPPING
1 can of coconut milk
1 tsp Bourbon vanilla powder
1 tbsp cane sugar
4 fresh figs
200 g blueberries
mint leaves

Heat oven to 125 °C. Put a parchment paper on a baking sheet. Using a pastry brush and sesame oil ”paint” a cake sized circle on the parchment paper. Spread the almond crumbs over the oil.

Whisk the egg whites in a steel bowl until they form stiff peeks. Whisk in the sugar little by little. Whisk until the meringue looks glossy, then add the corn flour and vinegar.

Spread the meringue dough inside the circle on top of the almond crumbs. Bake for 1.5 hours on the lowest rack of the oven. Let the meringue cool down.

Store the coconut milk can in a fridge. Open the can from the bottom. Pour the liquid out and scoop the thick cream into a bowl. Add cane sugar and vanilla. Mix the cream until it becomes fluffy.

Spread the whipped coconut cream on the cooled merigue right before serving. Cut the figs in quarters. Decorate the cake with figs, blueberries and mint leaves. If you like almonds you can use them for the decoration as well.
